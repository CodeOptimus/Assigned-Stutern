My Favourite Meal:

Waakye

Origin:
Waakye originates from Northern Ghana. A typical Ghanian cuisine where meals usually consists of a starchy component, sauce and a protein.

Ingredients:

Ingredients Measurements
Dried black eyed peas, soaked overnight 1 Cup
White Rice 2 Cups
Dried millet stalk leaves 10 pieces
Water for cooking As needed
Salt As needed to taste

Steps to Prepare:

  1. Fill a pot with about 4 cups of water.
  2. Add the millet leaf stalks
  3. Boil the water and millet leaf over high heat.(The millet leaves will begin to turn the water a deep red color.)
  4. Rinse the soaked beans and add the boiling water.
  5. Cover the beans let it cook until it is tender.
  6. Add water by the cup if the beans dry out before they are soft.
  7. Rinse the rice until the water is clear and add to the soft beans.
  8. Add the salt and more water if needed.
  9. Stir the pot and replace the cover. (Lower the heat and simmer the rice until it is cooked thouroghly and all the water has absorbed.)
  10. Turn off the heat and let the pot rest for 5 minutes.
  11. Remove the millet leaves fluff the rice and beans with a fork.
